Postage Stamps

About Postage Stamps

A postage stamp is a small rectangle of paper issued by a post office. They are glued to the face of any mail or envelope which is then processed by the post office where a postmark (indicating the date and origin) is applied to the left and right sides of the mail.


They are sold by the USPS and other retailers.

How to Mail to your Loved Ones

  1. Write a thoughtful handwritten letter and seal it in an envelope
  2. Choose a stamp you like from your local post office
  3. Place it on the top right-hand corner of the envelope
  4. Remember their address or look it up and write it in the center
  5. Now jot down your own address (return address) in the top left corner
  6. Drop it off at your local post office or collection box
  7. Voila! Your letter will be processed and delievered to their mailbox shortly
